Biography

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute of Electrical Machines, RWTH Aachen University. My research focuses on electrical machines, insulation systems, high-frequency modeling, condition monitoring, and intelligent health management of electrified systems.

I received my Ph.D. degree from RWTH Aachen University in 2022. My research experience covers fuel-cell hybrid energy systems, high-frequency modeling of electrical machines, insulation material testing, lifetime prediction, and fault diagnosis of electrical drives.

Research Interests

Electrical Machines & Drives

Design, modeling, optimization, and analysis of electrical machines and drive systems.

High-Frequency Modeling

White-box and grey-box modeling of machines, cables, and drive systems for EMC and overvoltage analysis.

Insulation Systems

Aging mechanisms, partial discharge, lifetime prediction, and reliability of machine insulation systems.

Condition Monitoring

Online monitoring, fault diagnosis, digital twins, and machine-learning-based prognostics.

Electric Aviation

High-power-density electric propulsion systems and health-aware design for future aircraft.
